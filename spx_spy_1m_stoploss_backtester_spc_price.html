SPX 0DTE Stop-Loss Backtester

SPY 1-min bars × 10 ≈ synthetic SPX  |  short put / short call / iron condor (wings ignored)  |  sweep entry time × margin × stop distance
1 — Data file
Expected columns: idx, tdate, open, high, low, close, volume, barCount, average. tdate format YYYYMMDD HH:MM:SS, timestamps are 2 hours behind EST — this is corrected automatically. Price is multiplied ×10 to approximate SPX. Bars outside 9:30–16:00 EST are dropped.
No file loaded.
2 — Entry times (EST)
3 — Position type
Long wings are ignored throughout — only the short strikes are modeled.
4 — Margin (strike distance from entry price, in SPX points)
5 — Stop / threshold distance (points before the short strike)
Stop level = strike + threshold (put) / strike − threshold (call). If price touches that level intraday before close, the leg is closed for a loss of threshold points (proxy). If never touched, the leg is held to the close.
6 — Assumed credit per winning leg
We have no historical option premiums, only the underlying. To compare wins and losses on the same scale, every leg that survives to the close untouched and finishes out-of-the-money is scored as +this many points. Set this to whatever average credit you'd realistically collect for the margin sizes you're testing — it scales the whole table, not the ranking logic.
